Explanation

The Euclidean Algorithm is a relatively easy way to determine the greatest common divisor of two numbers (the GCD). We can prove that we have the correct divisor using a certificate of correctness, which takes the form as+bt=d, where a and b are given values, d=gcd(a,b), and s and t are specific integers. We call it the certificate of correctness since s and t are all we need to 'certify' that we have the right number.

Finsing the GCD is pretty easy using the base Euclidean Algorithm, but finding the certificates of correctness require lots of substitution. We can reduce the work needed using the Extended Euclidean Algorithm, which can get us the GCD, along with the certificates of correcteness to form the equation as+bt=d
